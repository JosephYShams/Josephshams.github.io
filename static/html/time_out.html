Below, you will find all the smart home devices you have purchased, with the relevant configuration behaviours for each device capability.

Smart TV

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart TV

  • Connectedness: Sometimes the only way to protect your privacy is to disconnect your smart TV from the internet, losing the smart functionalities. So it is recommended that you get used to using your smart TV without connecting it to the Internet.
  • Learning: Turn off automatic content recognition to stop smart TV's learning what shows you like to watch.
  • Voice Control: TV's with microphones constantly listen so they can react when you use voice commands to control your TV or perform a search. When discussing personal information, stand as far away from smart TV remote controls as they usually have a built-in microphone

Smart Clock

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Clock

  • Connectedness: Disconnecting smart clocks from the internet will disable all smart features, as well as stop any data being collected..
  • Sensing (Learning): To stop your smart clock learning what time you wake up/go to sleep, turn off learning mode If this is not possible, then occasionally swap between smart and non-smart clocks.
  • Delegation (Automation): Smart clocks that listen for voice commands try to automate your alarms. To stop this, mute/ turn off the built-in microphones

Smart Alarm

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Alarm

  • Connectedness: For smart alarms to work, they must be connected to the Internet. This allows for continuous data collection. To increase privacy, disconnect smart alarms from the internet when not in use (i.e., when you are home).
  • Remote Control: To stop smart alarms learning and inferring information about you, do not connect virtual assistants (such as Amazon Alexa).
  • Voice Control: Remote controlling your smart devices can be dangerous (especially if you are connecting through open/insecure networks). To reduce privacy risks, setup 2 Factor Authentication (2FA).

Smart Fridge

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Fridge

  • Connectedness: Smart fridges, like all smart devices, require constant Internet connections. This can lead to unwanted data collection. To stop this, disconnect smart fridges from the internet when the smart features are not being used.
  • Sensing (Learning): Smart fridges can learn and inform you when food is running low or close to its expiration date, or suggesting recipes depending on the food in your fridge. This information could be collected and used against your wishes.
  • Remote ControlThe information that smart fridges collect could be negatively used against you. To reduce this risk, do not remote control your smart fridge when connected to open/public networks.

Smart Thermostat

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Thermostat

  • Connectedness: To reduce the change of advertisers collecting data about how smart thermostats are used, disconnect the device from the internet when its smart functionalities are not needed.
  • Learning (Sensing): Turn off learning mode. For example, turn off auto-schedule learning on a Nest thermostat to stop your thermostat making adjustments to its temperature schedule and to stop the device from learning what temperatures you like and when you like them.
  • Remote Control: The information collected by smart thermostats can be used to learn when your home is unoccupied. To reduce the risks of data leakage, do not remotely control your smart thermostat using open/public networks.

Smart Speaker

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Speaker

  • Connectedness: Smart speakers are designed to learn about you and perform tasks on your behalf, this can have privacy risks! To reduce the threats imposed, disconnect your smart speaker from the internet.
  • Sensing (Learning): Over prolonged use, smart speakers learn what your optimal music volume is, for example, raising and lowering the volume to match your previous usage patterns.
  • Voice Control: To preserve privacy and limit personal or sensitive data from being learned about you, place smart speakers away from populated areas of your home.

Smart Light

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Light

  • Connectedness: Smart lights are almost always connected to the internet, making it difficult to control what data is collected. If you purchase a smart light that only connects to the internet when the blub is lit up, the data collections could be reduced to an acceptable level.
  • Sensing (Learning): Creating a profile allows for your smart light to automate how you use the device over time. If you want to reduce how much data smart lights collect about you, do not create profiles.
  • Delegation (Automation): Smart lights learn how often you turn lights on and off, creating a pattern of when your home is occupied. To reduce this, disable the learning mode through the accompanying apps. (You may need to find resources online to help you).

Smart Camera

The following are configuration behaviours you can perform to limit the privacy risks associated with a Smart Camera

  • Connectedness: To reduce the risk of unauthorised access to your smart cameras, disconnect your smart cameras from the internet when you are home.
  • Sensing (Learning): If you cannot disconnect your camera from the internet, facing the camera away from populated areas is the next best step to reducing unwanted data collection and learning.
  • Remote Control: Smart cameras can record and store personal and sensitive data (such as video/audio feeds). If an open/public network is attacked, and you connect to the attacked network to remote control your smart camera, there is a risk that your camera feeds could be accessed by unauthorised parties.
